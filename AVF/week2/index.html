Week 1

Week 1 Discussion : Industry Research, Project Intent 

Less obvious perks of using 'live' data resources include logins and payments. How could Twitter be used in these two scenarios?

Twitter’s secure login feature could be used to validate user IDs in apps that utilize financial transactions.

 

Visit Programmableweb.com and search for JSON-P data sources. Why would we need JSON-P, specifically?

JSON-P allows us to create cross-domain requests. In other words, we can draw data from other domains, run the data through a function, and pass the data as a JSON object on a different site.

 

Based on the JSON-P search results: what data resource(s) do you think might pair well with which types of Native device features?

The notifications and contacts are a natural pairing with the Twitter or Tumblr APIs. An alert or breaking news from your app can be sent to selected people in Contacts. The camera and geolocation pair well together with APIs that have a “check in” social component, like FourSquare or a photo feature such as the Flickr or Instagram APIs.

 

Please describe any design and functionality intentions you may have for your Demo app components this month.

I would like to integrate maps, geolocation, camera and notifications into my “Cryptid Keeper” app. The app is supposed to allow the user to record the sighting of a cryptid, like a sasquatch, or chupacabra, so the features I mentioned would fit well.

 

Briefly describe some past 'lessons learned' regarding workflow as it relates to research, design, development, etc.

The number one lesson learned for me is to manage my time so that I can ask questions before the assignment is due. I research apps by searching through the iTunes store for apps with similar functionality so I can get a sense of what features potential competitors are offering.

Sometimes I come up with a design by taking screen captures of interfaces I like and rearranging them in Photoshop, and sometimes I just sketch out my ideas on paper with markers. If I’m stuck, I look at the O’Reilly mobile design patterns book to remind myself of industry-standard best practices.

I hate flow charts, so I don’t bother with that anymore. Instead I make storyboards to work out the interactivity and workflow.
