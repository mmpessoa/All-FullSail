Week 1

Week 1 Discussion : Industry Research, Project Intent 

What types of native features (on Android and iOS devices) would you be able to implement with the use of HTML/CSS/JS only?

Web apps can use the following features utilizing HTML, CSS and JS: Geolocation, Device Rotation, Device Orientation (accelerometer, magnetometer, gyroscope, compass), Touch Gestures (swipe, pinch, zoom), Offline Storage, access to Contacts, Notifications and Camera access.

What types of features do you think will pair well together in an app?

The notifications and contacts are a natural pairing. An alert or breaking news from your app can be sent to selected people in Contacts. The camera and geolocation pair well together in apps that have a “check in” social component, like FourSquare. Device Orientation and Touch Gestures pair well in game apps, especially racing games.

Please describe any design and functionality intentions you may have for your Demo app components this month.

I would like to integrate maps, geolocation, camera and notifications into my “Cryptid Keeper” app. The app is supposed to allow the user to record the sighting of a cryptid, like a sasquatch, or chupacabra, so the features I mentioned would fit well.

Briefly describe some past 'lessons learned' regarding workflow as it relates to research, design, development, etc.

The number one lesson learned for me is to manage my time so that I can ask questions before the assignment is due. I research apps by searching through the iTunes store for apps with similar functionality so I can get a sense of what features potential competitors are offering.

Sometimes I come up with a design by taking screen captures of interfaces I like and rearranging them in Photoshop, and sometimes I just sketch out my ideas on paper with markers. If I’m stuck, I look at the O’Reilly mobile design patterns book to remind myself of industry-standard best practices.

I hate flow charts, so I don’t bother with that anymore. Instead I make storyboards to work out the interactivity and workflow.

Week 2 Discussion: Accessibility 

Read through the information provided in this Discussion and refer to your research as you answer the following questions:

  1. What are some of the ways that you could change your HTML on your Demo app to be more accessible? (Remember, you can use the Section tag with jQuery, for instance)

  2. Visit a video service on the web (like YouTube) and search for "Screen Reader Demo" and "Mobile Screen Reader" - what are some of the common frustrations (or common support mechanisms) that you see among the examples that are given - how could you fix them (or use them)?

  3. Devices: Please list two devices (and some of their features) that are made specifically to support Accessibility.

  4. Mobile Apps: Please list two apps (either Android or iOS, or both) that are made specifically to support Accessibility.

Week 3  Discussion: Market Research

For this activity, please reply to this discussion, answering the following questions:

  1. What are the 'top selling' app categories in the U.S.?

  2. What are the 'top selling' app categories in Japan?

  3. What are the 'top selling' app categories in Great Britain?

  4. Based on your three answers above, what might you infer about each of the markets listed?

  5. What percentage of Android users have the latest/greatest OS in the U.S? in Japan? in Great Britain? How might these differences impact development?

  6. What is the average cost and bandwidth limits per user (in general) in each of the three markets? How may this impact development?

  7. And, finally, what would be your suggested strategy in project topic and global market choice? Feel free to have a first and second choice.
